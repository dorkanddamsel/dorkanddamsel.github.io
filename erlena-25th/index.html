“Lou & Erl - 25 Years Strong”

a silver Milestone of endless Love

It all began on June 6, 1999. It wasn't a grand setup or some scripted fairytale—it was something much simpler, more real. My best friend and maid of honor, Michelle, introduced me to Angelo. She was dating Jocyl then, who happened to be Angelo's cousin. No fireworks, no courting, no flowers. Just a quiet beginning, one that didn’t seem extraordinary at the time—but changed our lives forever.

Then came June 18, 1999—my birthday. Angelo gave me a gift that would mark the start of everything: an engagement ring. No long talks about "what ifs," no dramatic gestures—just a ring, and the unspoken promise that we would build something real together. That same ring is now worn by our daughter, Angela—a beautiful reminder of where it all started.

Every Sunday after that, Angelo would accompany me to church. Those quiet mornings became our way of getting to know each other. No pressure, no rush—just shared moments and growing understanding.

By August 1999, it was time for the families to meet. It was a simple gathering, but it meant everything. And by October, the decision was made—we would get married. Not someday. Not "if everything works out." We just knew.

On April 29, 2000, we said our vows.

Just a few weeks later, on June 18, 2000—exactly a year after Angelo gave me the ring—we found out we were expecting twins. On February 5, 2001, Feyunie and Denver were born. And that’s when the real adventure—the terror and the wonder—truly began.

Angelo gave up his job to take care of our babies. He cooked, cleaned, and loved without question. I started teaching to support our growing family, and he stood by me every step of the way. Through sleepless nights and messy days, his love never wavered. He remained faithful. Steady. Mine.

On December 6, 2006, our family was completed with the birth of our youngest, Angela. Our little surprise. Our light.

Years flew by, and by 2019, the kids were off pursuing their dreams—some in Laguna, others in Manila. With the house a little quieter, we found ourselves in a season we never really had before: courtship. Finally, we got to be a "couple"—taking walks, talking over coffee, holding hands like teenagers. It felt new, but it was also built on years of laughter, struggles, and quiet understanding.

And the rest? The rest is history.

A Jubilee for the Ages

25 years isn’t nothing to sneeze at, folks. That’s 9,132 days — to put that into perspective, that’s 25 birthdays, a thousand hugs, a handful of kisses, a couple births, a number of graduations, friends made and friends lost – decisions made and decisions botched, from days of reading newspapers and listening to radios, to doing almost anything with a computer, and yes, mobile phones do currently count.. It’s a hell of a rollercoaster ride, and it is definitely not for anyone.

As for the blessed couple celebrating this momentous moment, I am both in awe and inspired in knowing you guys. Truly, tremendously remarkble! Cheers to celebrating more ahead. Congratulations!
